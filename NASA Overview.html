NASA Overview

Introduction to NASA

From the establishment of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) in 1958, through its advancements in space technology and travel in the Cold War, all eyes were on the U.S. space program during a time of great achievements not only for the country, but for the world. NASA’s lunar program, named the Apollo Program, was the program that brought the U.S. a step ahead of the Soviet Union in the race to set men on the moon.

The Apollo Program

The Apollo Program began in 1961 when President John F. Kennedy announced to a special joint session of Congress the goal of getting an American safely to the moon and back by the end of the decade, and asked for funding for the lunar program. The program ran for 11 years until NASA’s funding was cut, making Apollo 17 the final launch of the program.

Apollo Timeline

While the timeline of this project spans throughout the entire timeline of the Apollo Program, there are three dates which will be focused on:

1967: The Apollo 1 fire and what was planned to be the first launch of the program.

1969: The Apollo 11 launch: the mission that brought men to the moon.

1972: The Apollo 17 launch: the final launch of the program.
