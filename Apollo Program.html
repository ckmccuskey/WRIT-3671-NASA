The Apollo Emblems

About the Mission Patches

Worn by NASA astronauts since 1965, the mission patch is designed by the crew to embody the spirit and objectives of their launch. Typically bearing the name of each crew member, and always bearing the name of the mission, the patches are silkscreened on the spacesuits, and sewn onto the flight suits, recovery suits, jackets, and all other official NASA gear for each mission.

Target Audience of the Emblems

Being that the emblems are created by the crew, for NASA, within the U.S. government, and are widely visible by the public, there is an expansive target audience. First, the mission crew and other NASA compartments. The crews are the ones carrying out the missions, along with mission control and other areas within the Administration; so the emblems serve as a visual representation and reminder of their goals. Second, the U.S. government. With NASA being a government agency working with federal funding, these emblems also represent the intentions of the U.S. government. And third, during a time when the world was watching the race between the Soviet Union and the U.S., these Apollo emblems were projected not only to U.S. citizens, but to a world-wide public audience.
